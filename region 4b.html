Moriones Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

    • Instructions:

  • In a large mixing bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  • Add the flour, baking powder, salt, and vanilla extract. Mix well until a dough forms.
  • Divide the dough into small portions and shape each portion into a helmet-like shape.
  • Place the shaped cookies onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  • Bake at 350°F for 15-20 minutes or until lightly golden brown
  • Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool for 5 minutes before dusting with powdered sugar.

    • Description:

    Moriones cookies is a famous delicacy from Marinduque province,known for its unique shape and delicious taste.

    Adobo sa Gata

    Ingredients:

  • 1 kilo pork belly, chopped
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Instructions:

  • In a pan, brown the pork belly until golden brown.
  • Add the onion and garlic. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Pour in the vinegar and soy sauce. Add the bay leaves.
  • Simmer until the pork is tender and the liquid is reduced.
  • Add the coconut milk and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  • Season with salt and pepper
  • Serve with steamed rice

    • Description:

    Adobo sa Gata is a popular dish in Marinduque, made with pork cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and coconut milk. It has a rich and creamy flavor that is perfect for pairing with steamed rice.

    KINILAW

    Ingredients:

  • 1 kilo fresh fish or seafood (such as squid, shrimp, or tuna), cleaned and diced.
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup calamansi juice
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 5 pieces red chili peppers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix the fish or seafood, vinegar, and calamansi juice .
  • Add the onion, garlic, and chili peppers.
  • Season with salt and pepper.
  • Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

    • Description:

    Kinilaw is a popular appetizer in Marinduque which is made with fresh fish or seafood marinated in vinegar, calamansi juice, and spices. It's a refreshing and tangy dish that's perfect for summer..

    SARSA

    Ingredients:

  • 1/4 kg ulang (freshwater shrimp)
  • grated coconut meat
  • coconut milk
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, minced
  • Siling labuyo (bird’s eye chilli)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt

    • Instructions:

  • In a mortar (or blender), make a rough paste of all the ingredients (except the coconut cream)
  • Shape a coconut leaf (watch the video) or use a foil and put the rough paste
  • Boil in coconut milk for about 30 minutes

    • Description:

    Sarsa is a popular dish that originated in the province of Romblon in the Philippines. It is a savory sauce made with shrimp and spices that is usually served with grilled or fried fish or meat.

    SINAING NA TULINGAN

    Ingredients:

  • 1 kilo tulingan, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup salt
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Black pepper to taste

    • Instructions:

  • In a pot, layer the tulingan, onion, and garlic.
  • Sprinkle salt and black pepper in between the layers
  • Pour in the vinegar and water. Add the bay leaves.
  • Bring to a boil then simmer for 3-4 hours or until the tulingan is tender.
  • Serve with steamed rice

    • Description:

    Sinaing na Tulingan is a popular dish in Occidental Mindoro, made with tulingan (bullet tuna) slow-cooked in salt and vinegar until tender and flavorful.
